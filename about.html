Who am I?

I may also term it love. I clearly remember the day when my father came home bringing our first computer. I pressed the button and the instant the screen lit a spark came off and woke my imagination and inspired myriads of ideas.

When I was in the fifth form, I got initiated into web hosting, basic HTML, CSS and PHP. I was taking participation in maths and computing contests and somehow I kept feeling that this was my path of development. Along the recent years, I never stopped building up my skills further. I received prizes in many team contests organized by JuniorAchievementBulgaria and at the beginning of the tenth form school year I became a member of the robotics club at school. It was then that the LEGO Mindstorms platform offered to me an easy and understandable way to assemble robots, and subsequently I faced the challenge of working with ZumoRobots with Arduino and Pololu 3pi Robot regarding microprocessors, electricity and voltage; all issues we studied in the Physics classes. On 6th June 2016 our team won the First Prize in the 2D Labyrinth at 2016 Festival of Robotics and Mechatronics. Things within the frame of those platforms do have their limits of development and regarding categories of higher competitive level, I needed something new. I started designing my own robots, spent countless hours at the whiteboard and at the testing sites. That is how at the same contest in 2016 in the category LINE TRACKING my Usain Bolt brought me the Third Prize amidst competitors from Bulgaria and Romania.

At RuseCodeWeek 2016 I together with two other boys decided to be part of the event. We attended an online course carried out by openSAP and received LEGO Mindstorms certificates. That way I, as a lecturer at the local university, conducted a number of tasks that led to the realization of interesting ideas from children's thoughts.

The time spent in writing software developed my logical and creative way of thinking and programming in itself served as a foundation in contests to come. I was attending a number of courses and conferences such as the one held by Startup Factory where upon completion I was awarded a certificate of WEB development using PHP. The project I run there was titled Olble -an online customizable website builder. Besides, during the three-day hackathon Ruse Hack 2.0, I was able to realize to a considerable extent my idea for an online machine for management of family financial and business needs. Apart from the First Prize winning, I was also awarded two special prizes in category Design by WebVox and Programista. My online-based projects constitute a significant part of my works and the one I am currently engaged with is titled toppynews - a platform for personalized RSS news.

Currently I have run into difficulties finding motivated people, yet for me the key to success is the headlong teamwork and long-term activities. Large web projects require just that and for someone like me this poses an enormous obstacle. I do believe that the UK is the place where such ambitious people and bright minds come from all across the world. In addition, the competitive environment is of crucial importance in the age of technological innovation. Last, but not least in importance, with the latest cutting-edge academic knowledge my endeavors would certainly be brought to realization.

My inner eye beholds a world where there is an ever-growing demand for software - starting with electric automobiles and web applications and extending to IoT (Internet of Things) with the myriads of gadgets destined to join the global web. As for me, I take it that the software engineer proper is a kind of inventor, a person who believes in his or her ideas and realizes them. Elon Musk is one of that kind - solutions seeker and a down-to-earth businessman. With his enthusiasm and potential, he stands for the word inspiration to me. Actually, opportunities are waiting round each corner and I am adamant about making the best of them.
